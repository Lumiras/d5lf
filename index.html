About

Danger Laser Falcon is a group of four graduate students operating out of the Center for Emerging Media Design and Development at Ball State University.

In our work, we try to make things informative and interesting, while still remaining fun.

Of course, because we're a rather new group, we don't have a lot of work to show off yet, but trust us, watch this space for even more awesome stuff in the months/years/decades/centuries to come!

Elise Lockwood

Elise Lockwood is currently pursuing her master's at Ball State's Emerging Media Design and Development program. She graduated from DePauw University in 2013 where she won the Chad Kostel Memorial Award for creative writing and the Barbara Petty Award for theatre. Elise is also a playwright, and her work was mostly recently seen at IndyFringe's Short Play Festival. When she isn't studying, writing, or working with local theatre companies, Elise lives in Fishers with her dog, Marge, her mini-pig Harvey, and her partner Derek.

Jared Lynch

Jared Lynch is largely interested in storytelling. He has been actively writing fiction since he was nine and he is currently studying transmedia storytelling with the intention of becoming an Experience Designer. Consistent themes in his writing are cassette tapes, houses, ethereal spiders, birds, and metaphysical manipulation. He enjoys exploring other mediums in addition to writing, and he is incredibly interested in the future of storytelling.

Sean May

Sean May is a writer, front-end web designer, video game designer, and a designer of a lot of other things, too many to mention here. His focus in the EMDD master's program is transmedia production. His interests are video games, music, and graphic design. He lives with his wife, Jeni, and a cat, Sophie.

Muncie Has Magic

Muncie Has Magic was our first project as a group. It lead participants through a single street of downtown Muncie, IN on a tour that begins like any other audio tour, but quickly takes a turn for the absurd.

Make Me

Make Me is a project examining gender roles, specifically how people are told they are supposed to dress and portray themselves outwardly. We also examine the negative effects those kinds of stereotypes have on people every day.

For more information on Make Me, go to Makeme.gallery

  • Client: Center for EMDD
  • Date: December 2015
  • Service: Interactive Art Installation and website
